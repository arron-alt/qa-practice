How AI is changing the landscape of QA

From AI assistants to local agents, AI is reshaping how quality gets done. Here are a few of the tools leading the shift.

In short

AI is reshaping QA by taking on the legwork — drafting test cases from a design, turning behaviours into structured checks, generating bug reports and ticket comments, and inspecting pages for errors in seconds.

But the foundations still come first: knowing what to test, where the risks are, and what the user journeys should be is human work — and it’s exactly what makes the AI useful in the first place.

Used that way, AI isn’t a replacement for QA judgement; it’s a force multiplier that lets a small team cover far more ground without ever taking its hand off the wheel.

× What each agent does Reads the brief, pulls the rules — acceptance criteria, components, colours, spacing — and drafts the test cases. QA verifies before anything ships.
× When the AI finds an issue If a live check fails — or the live build doesn’t match Figma — the AI flags it, logs it to the data pool, and passes it back to QA. The cycle repeats until it’s right.
